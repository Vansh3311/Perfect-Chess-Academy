Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about our chess coaching

🎯 Getting Started

We welcome students from age 5 onwards. Our beginner courses are designed specifically for young learners, introducing chess through fun, game-based activities that build foundational skills naturally.

For in-person classes, all materials, including chess sets, are provided. For online classes, we recommend having a standard chess set for practice, but it's not mandatory to start. We can recommend good quality, affordable sets.

We offer flexible options for missed classes. Students can attend a make-up session with another batch (subject to availability) or access a recorded video of the session's key lessons. Please inform us at least 24 hours in advance if you plan to miss a class.

Progress is tracked through a combination of regular in-class assessments, puzzle-solving performance, practice game analysis, and official rating changes from tournaments. We provide parents with a detailed progress report every three months.

Absolutely! We strongly encourage competitive play. We host our own internal monthly tournaments and guide students on participating in local, state, and national level FIDE-rated events. Our "Tournament Preparation" course is dedicated to this.

Group classes provide a collaborative learning environment and are great for learning fundamentals and practicing with peers. Private coaching offers a highly personalized, one-on-one training plan tailored to a student's specific strengths, weaknesses, and goals, leading to accelerated improvement.

Yes! While many of our students are children, we have dedicated batches and private coaching options for adult learners of all levels, from complete beginners to experienced club players looking to improve their rating.

Yes, we offer a comprehensive online coaching program with live interactive classes, online playing platforms, and digital access to all our study materials. This allows students from anywhere in the world to learn with us.

The monthly course fee includes the scheduled number of classes, all digital study materials and workbooks, access to our online practice portal, and participation in our internal academy tournaments.

Progress in chess varies greatly depending on the individual's dedication, practice time, and natural aptitude. However, with consistent attendance and practice (2-3 hours per week), most beginners see a significant improvement and can play confidently in tournaments within 6-8 months.
